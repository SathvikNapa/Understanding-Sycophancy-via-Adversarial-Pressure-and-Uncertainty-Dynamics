\begin{abstract}
Sycophancy in large language models is a well-documented failure. While prior work has established that LLMs change their stance under social pressure, the effect of a model's pre-pressure uncertainty on susceptibility has not been explored. In our approach, we understand the effect of behavioral certainty, measured with repeated sampling, on model belief capitulation under multi-turn conversation of escalating pressure through varied prompts. We experiment with GPT, Claude, and Gemini families of models across capability tiers on MMLU-Pro, GPQA-Diamond, and Humanity's Last Exam (HLE). We find that questions which induce uncertainty flip at twice the rate of certain questions. We additionally find that escalating pressure level, interacting with pre-pressure uncertainty, is a significant flip predictor. Third, under escalating pressure models can be classified into two groups, which we refer to as ``resisters'' and ``capitulators''. One key takeaway is that susceptibility to sycophancy is model-specific and partially moderated by behavioral certainty, operationalized as the entropy of the model's answer distribution under repeated sampling before any pressure is applied.
\end{abstract}
