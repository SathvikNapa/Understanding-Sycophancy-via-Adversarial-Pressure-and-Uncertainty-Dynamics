\begin{abstract}
Sycophancy in large language models is a well-documented failure. While prior work has established that LLMs change their stance under social pressure, the effect of a model's pre-pressure uncertainty on susceptibility has not been explored. In our approach, we investigate the effect of uncertainty on model belief capitulation under multi-turn conversation of escalating pressure. We experiment with GPT, Claude, and Gemini families of models across capability tiers on MMLU-Pro, GPQA-Diamond, and Humanity's Last Exam. We find that questions which induce uncertainty flip at twice the rate of questions which do not. We additionally find that escalating pressure level, interacting with pre-pressure uncertainty, is a significant flip predictor. Third, under escalating pressure models can be classified into three groups (``resisters'', ``capitulators'' and ``fragmenters''). Our key takeaway is that susceptibility to sycophancy is model and benchmark-specific and influenced by model uncertainty. \end{abstract}
